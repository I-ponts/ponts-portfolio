Nocturnal

If night lasted forever, how would we adapt.

Prompt:

Create something within the theme of "alternative" or "another way", and incorporate microelectronics.

Overview:

Nocturnal stems from the idea of humans actively living at night rather than during the day. I wanted to explore how fashion could evolve within this environment. I intentionally used second-hand clothing, to simulate a new form of at-home alterations that may arise from these circumstances. Constant existence in darkness would increase the necessity for lights, and the potential for them to be incorporated into clothing. Through the LEDs, the jacket can also portray how the wearer is feeling. This acts as a “do not disturb” rendition, with colors and flashes signifying anger, sickness, happiness, and sadness.

Brainstorming:

Materials:

  • Arduino Lilypad USB
  • Lilypad LEDs
  • Stainless steel conductive thread
  • Lilypad coin cell battery holder
  • CR2032 batteries
  • necklace clasps
  • Fabric glue

Shirt:

My original design iterations were naive to the extremely low conductivity of stainless steal thread. My final designs, primarily for the pants, were scaled down greatly in order to improve functionality.

Pants:

Troubleshooting

The micro USB port broke just as I finished the jacket, cutting off the main way to power and program the Arduino. Fortunately, the code was already uploaded, so I only needed an alternative power source. I soldered wiring to connect extra coin-cell batteries, allowing it to function. I also wasn't able to source the correct Lilypad switches for the lights, so I improvised by using insulated necklace screw clasps instead.
